## Absurdism

I have yet to read Camus, but my understanding of absurdism is the idea that searching for meaning need-not end in a nihilistic answer (there is no meaning), or endless meaning-seeking or unfalsifiable assumptions.

Many meaning-searchers either give up and become nihilists or pursue meaning-creation to satisfy their unrealistic desires. On the other hand, what if we simply abandon the question all-together: it was never a valid question to begin with!

Why not cherish personal meaning (and a seeming lack of cosmic meaning) instead?

## A Posteriori Jargon: Meaning as a Social Concept that Needs Identity Signaling

So, how do we create personal meaning? One solution is to cherish shared experiences by creating mutual language, art, and representations: the formation of an *identity*.

### Aesthetic Compression: Commoditizing Experiences via Styles

The irony of representing a collection of shared experiences in terms of jargon and language is that it makes it far easier to commoditize! In time, it is easily converted into an aesthetic: one that can be bought, performed, promoted, misused, and overproduced into incoherent slop.

If they were so easily broken, were they ever meaningful anyway? I mean, at least they make people feel something!

## Meaning and Coherence

I sometimes wonder why we mark incoherence as more wholesome. Avoiding over-interpretation can feel less manipulative and more "authentic". And not imposing a deep structure allows projection and feels inclusive and safe.

Is meaning something that only appears as local coherence signals but cannot be reasonably sustained across a network?
